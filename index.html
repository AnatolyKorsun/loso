features

Narrow and tall bottles

Similar types of wine bottles are used to store such wines. The bottom of these bottles may be flat or with a slight internal bulge. Also, oak barrels can be an ideal option.

Vintage

Grapes, more than any other product, affect the quality of the wine. And the quality of the berry itself depends on the growing season, weather, soil, acidity, time of collection and method of pruning.

Grape processing

The process of separating grapes from stems is called processing. Some winemakers leave small fragments of the stem to increase wine tannin. This can be done manually or mechanically.

Fermentation

After the fermentation process has begun. According to its results, grape juice turns into alcohol. And it turns into carbon dioxide (as well as ethanol). A very important point in this process is the fermentation temperature and its speed.

Purification

After fermentation and processing, the stage of purification and filtration. Here, any solid particles that remain after fermentation are removed. To remove residues, winemakers transfer wine from one vessel to another through a filter.

Bottling

After aging, the wine is bottled. During the bottling process, additional doses of sulfite are added to prevent further fermentation in the bottle. Then the bottles are sealed with a stopper or screw cap.
